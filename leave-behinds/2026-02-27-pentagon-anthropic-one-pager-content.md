## Document — "Congress Must Act on Military AI Guardrails"

**Header:** "URGENT: Pentagon Deadline of 5:01 PM, Friday, February 27, 2026"

**Attribution at footer:** Daniel Parshall, Ph.D. | AI Safety Researcher | Maryland Constituent | Former physicist & data scientist | parshall.dan+ai@gmail.com | (302) 455-7299

### THE SITUATION (factual claims)
- SecDef Hegseth issued ultimatum to Anthropic: remove all safety restrictions by 5:01 PM Friday or face supply-chain-risk designation and/or DPA invocation
- Anthropic was the first AI company deployed on classified military networks
- Anthropic's two redlines:
  - No mass surveillance of American citizens (4th Amendment + statutory)
  - No autonomous lethal weapons without human judgment (consistent with DoD Directive 3000.09's "appropriate levels of human judgment over the use of force")
- Pentagon originally agreed to these terms in the original $200M contract; was unilaterally attempting to alter terms under duress

### WHY THIS MATTERS (analytical claims)
1. **Logical contradiction** — Pentagon simultaneously threatened "supply chain risk" designation (=keep out) AND DPA invocation (=compel access); these are mutually inconsistent, suggesting threats are punitive not substantive
2. **Technical reality** — current AI hallucination rates are acceptable for text generation but catastrophic for kill decisions; HITL is engineering necessity not bureaucratic obstacle
3. **Precedent** — if executive can use Korean War-era industrial mobilization statute to strip safety guardrails, no AI company will maintain safety redlines under government pressure again
4. **Constitutional stakes** — unrestricted AI surveillance + autonomous lethal capability without congressional authorization = fundamental shift in government-citizen power balance

### IMMEDIATE ACTIONS REQUESTED (the explicit asks — four of them)
1. Issue a public statement today that Congress is watching and will investigate any invocation of DPA or supply-chain designation against Anthropic
2. Contact SecDef directly to communicate that congressional oversight of military AI use is forthcoming
3. Demand hearings before Armed Services and Intelligence Committees on Pentagon's use of AI in classified systems, including HITL requirements for lethal operations
4. Initiate legislation codifying HITL requirements for autonomous weapons and prohibiting warrantless AI-enabled mass surveillance of U.S. persons

### Closing pull-quote
*"The American people should not have to rely on a private company to be the last line of defense for their constitutional rights and the rule of law. That is Congress's job."*
