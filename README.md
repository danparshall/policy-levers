# policy_levers

Identify and prioritize education and engagement of U.S. policy makers (Congress, federal agencies, regulatory bodies) on AI economic impact and safety issues.

See `CLAUDE.md` for project scope, structure, and conventions.

## Organizational context

This work is conducted under **Canary Institute**, a 501(c)(3) nonpartisan public-interest organization (in formation). Two activity categories matter for tax status, and project materials should be reviewed against this line:

### Always permissible (unlimited)

General education and nonpartisan analysis on AI's impact across jobs, defense, civil society, and the long-term future. This includes:

- Briefing staff on the technical landscape of AI capabilities and risks
- Discussing the policy options Congress could consider
- Providing factual analysis of specific bills in response to questions
- Publishing research, op-eds, and explainers
- Building relationships with offices to be available as an ongoing technical resource

### Lobbying (capped at 20% of expenditures under the 501(h) election)

Direct communications to legislators or staff asking for support of, opposition to, or legislative action on a specific bill. This includes:

- "Please cosponsor S.2938"
- "Please request a Commerce hearing on S.2938"
- Grassroots calls asking the public to contact their senator about a specific bill

### Practical implication for this repo

Internal strategic notes (senators.yaml, followups.yaml) may track which bills are in play, which staffers know what, and what would unblock specific bills — that's analysis, not communication. The line gets drawn at *what is actually said to staff*. Planned communications should be reviewed before delivery so that direct-lobbying communications stay strategic and rare. Most outreach should be educational; specific-bill asks are reserved for high-leverage moments.
