## Why ControlAI matters here

ControlAI is a UK-based AI safety advocacy org. Leticia García is their
Policy Advisor and author of the LessWrong post "What We Learned from
Briefing 70+ Lawmakers on the Threat from AI" (May 2025):
https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/Xwrajm92fdjd7cqnN/what-we-learned-from-briefing-70-lawmakers-on-the-threat

Key facts from García's post:
- Briefed 70+ UK parliamentarians Q4 2024 – mid-May 2025
- 1 in 3 lawmakers they met chose to take a public stance endorsing
  their campaign statement (that AI poses extinction risk)
- Their policy ask: independent AI regulator, licensing regime
  (training license for >10^25 FLOP, compute license with KYC for
  cloud providers >10^17 FLOP/s, application license for downstream
  developers), prohibit specific behaviors (superintelligent AI,
  unbounded AI, external system access, recursive self-improvement)
- In replies: "We've already had some meetings with US Congressional
  offices. We are currently growing our team in the US and expect to
  ramp up our efforts in the coming months."

Dan's relevance:
- DC-based, willing to do Hill work
- Physics PhD, technical credibility
- Two prior Hill visits (standing start, no sustained relationships yet)
- 10K+ hours science communication / translation experience
- Centrist, willing to be direct about catastrophic risk without
  leading with it
- Has a defensible track record (CDR and BCAI papers) to bring into
  briefings

ControlAI is expanding into the US. Dan is in DC with relevant skills.
This is a plausible match for either employment or structured
collaboration.

## Three plausible outreach framings

### Framing 1: Employment/contract inquiry

"I saw your LessWrong post about briefing 70+ UK parliamentarians. I'm
in DC, pivoting into AI policy full-time, and saw you mentioned
expanding US operations. My background [physics PhD, data science,
CDR+BCAI papers, Corda fellow, 10K hours of science communication] is
exactly the translational Hill-engagement profile your US ramp-up likely
needs. Happy to share CV and discuss."

Honest. Direct. Works if they're hiring.

### Framing 2: Methodology comparison / collaboration

"I've just read your post on the UK briefing program and found it
unusually useful — the treatment of inferential distance, the split
between live-meeting framing and the underlying ask, and the discipline
around tangible-vs-polite feedback are all lessons I want to internalize
before my next round of Hill engagement. I'm a DC-based researcher (ex-
physicist, CDR paper on AI labor impact, BCAI paper on alignment-as-
governance) and wondered whether you'd be open to a short call to
compare notes. Particularly interested in what you'd do differently
adapting the approach to Congress vs. Parliament."

Lower ask. Builds relationship without pressuring for a role. Creates
natural opening for role conversation later.

### Framing 3: Offer to co-brief

"I'm planning a Senate ENR office briefing on the energy infrastructure
implications of frontier AI and wondered if ControlAI would be
interested in joining or co-sponsoring. Your policy ask on licensing
frontier compute has direct implications for grid and siting decisions
that ENR has jurisdiction over. If you're ramping up US engagement, a
joint briefing with a physicist constituent might be worth the time
both ways."

Higher specificity, higher commitment. Only send this if Dan actually
has a scheduled or near-scheduled ENR meeting.

### Recommended framing

Framing 2 is the best opening move. Lowest friction, highest
information yield. Dan learns whether ControlAI is interested in
engagement with him, and learns more about their actual US plans,
before committing to anything specific. Either converts to Framing 1
(employment) or Framing 3 (co-briefing) once the relationship is
established.

## Specific questions Dan should surface in any conversation

1. What does ControlAI's US ramp-up actually look like? Headcount plans,
   timeline, geographic base, funding status.
2. Who's leading the US effort? (García herself? A new US hire? Someone
   existing moving over?)
3. What's their US policy ask going to be — same as UK (licensing +
   specific prohibitions) or adapted?
4. Do they have interest in taking on co-branded researchers or only
   staff?
5. How do they think about messaging discipline with US audiences
   specifically? (The UK AI Safety Institute / AISI rebrand to AI
   Security Institute under Labour provided a handle they used; there's
   no directly analogous US handle.)
6. What's their relationship to other US AI safety advocacy orgs —
   AIPN, ARI, CAIS AF, Alliance for Secure AI? Coalition members,
   competitors, neutral?

## What to push back on if it comes up

Dan has said he wants to be able to be direct about catastrophic risk
but doesn't want to be defined by it on Canary's public positioning.
ControlAI's UK approach leads with the CAIS extinction statement as a
live-meeting opener (1-in-3 endorsement rate from MPs). Dan's target
voice is quieter on the public-facing side.

If ControlAI wants him to be their US face doing the same extinction-
statement lead, that's a specific commitment he should consider
carefully. It probably works better as a live-meeting move than as a
public-positioning move, which matches his preferred split. But he
should not drift into becoming ControlAI's branded-pose representative
without thinking about what that costs for his independent voice on CDR
and Canary's broader work.

Worth raising with ControlAI explicitly: "How would a US collaboration
work if I want to lead with economic/labor framing for some audiences
and extinction framing for others, depending on who's in the room?"
Their answer tells Dan a lot.
