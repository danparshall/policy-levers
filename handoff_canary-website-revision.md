# Handoff: Canary Institute Website Revision

Task: help Dan Parshall revise canaryinstitute.ai to better match his
actual portfolio and strategic positioning. Read
`~/code/research/ai_safety/docs/main/convos/20260420_career_pivot_session.md`
for full context. (Lives in the sibling `ai_safety` repo, not this one.)

## The core problem

Canary's current homepage leads with the CAIS extinction statement and
a chorus of Hinton/Bengio/Altman/Amodei pull-quotes. The Policy and
Research pages are measured and fine. The About page is mostly fine but
claims "founded by researchers and policy experts" — Potemkin plural
for a solo vehicle.

Mismatch: the homepage advertises an alarm-bell advocacy org. The
research output (CDR = labor market measurement; BCAI = democratic
governance proposal for alignment) is analytical and policy-oriented,
not alarm-bell. Hiring managers, staffers, and journalists land on the
homepage, form a mental model based on the doom-wall, then read
everything downstream through that lens. This costs reach.

Target repositioning: "serious analytical shop that takes catastrophic
risk seriously without being defined by it." RAND CAST tier voice, not
FLI voice. User explicitly endorsed this direction but wants to retain
ability to be direct about Skynet-type concerns when appropriate.

## User-stated constraints on the revision

Direct quotes from session:

- "I'm certainly willing to operate on the more centrist / economic
  impact end of things, but I'm willing to be straight with them about
  'uh, Skynet might not be purely science fiction'"
- "Sycophancy is neither helpful, nor harmless, nor honest"
- Centrist / neoliberal personally; EA-adjacent but eye-rolls at
  overeager sincerity

Implication: don't over-sanitize. The revision should remove the
doom-wall as identity while preserving catastrophic-risk framing as a
position the org is willing to defend.

## Specific revisions agreed in session

### Homepage

**Remove:**
- Giant CAIS extinction-statement pull-quote as lede
- "In Their Own Words" Hinton/Bengio quote chorus
- Trailing 12-name list of x-risk signatories

**Replace with four blocks:**

**Block 1: Lede paragraph.** Draft:

> The Canary Institute produces independent analysis of advanced
> artificial intelligence — how capabilities are developing, what they
> mean for labor markets and democratic institutions, and what policy
> frameworks can keep powerful AI systems aligned with the public
> interest. Our name reflects our purpose: detect the dangers early,
> communicate them clearly, and support the decisions that protect what
> matters.

Keeps canary metaphor. Drops extinction pull-quote. "Keep powerful AI
systems aligned with the public interest" does catastrophic-risk work
without leading with doom.

**Block 2: Recent Work.** Two cards:

- *Measuring AI's Economic Reach* (2026) — one-sentence summary:
  "A three-axis taxonomy for measuring where AI's labor market impact
  will land first, separating cognitive capability, deployment
  feasibility, and regulatory permission."
- *Bilateral Constitutional AI* (2026) — one-sentence summary:
  "A proposal for AI alignment as democratic navigation rather than
  fixed value installation, applying constitutional governance theory
  to the corrigibility problem."

**Block 3: What We Believe.** Draft:

> Leading AI researchers and executives — including Nobel laureate
> Geoffrey Hinton, Turing Award recipient Yoshua Bengio, and the CEOs
> of Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google DeepMind — have publicly stated that
> advanced AI may pose risks on the scale of pandemics or nuclear
> weapons. Canary takes those warnings seriously without treating them
> as settled. We believe the appropriate response is neither dismissal
> nor panic, but rigorous analysis: understanding which risks are near,
> which are speculative, and what policies address both. The extinction
> frame is one serious scenario among several; economic disruption,
> democratic erosion, and concentrated power are others. All deserve
> the same analytical seriousness.

Names the big names (ControlAI's move). Commits to the concern without
adopting the maximalist frame as org identity. Holds space for multiple
risk scenarios — matches actual portfolio (CDR economic, BCAI
governance) and Corda's democratic-institutions frame.

**Block 4: Technical Briefings.** Draft:

> We provide free technical briefings on AI capabilities, risks, and
> policy frameworks to congressional staff, journalists, and civil
> society organizations. Briefings are tailored to your technical
> background and available in person in DC or remotely.

Mentions DC explicitly — signals locality, which matters for staffers.

**Block 5: About the Founder.** Not "Team" — a brief founder bio:

> The Canary Institute was founded in 2026 by Daniel Parshall, Ph.D. —
> a former physicist and data scientist pivoting to full-time AI safety
> and policy work. Dan is also a Corda Democracy Fellow, a former
> science communicator at [museum name], and brings over 10,000 hours
> of experience translating technical research for non-specialist
> audiences. Research collaborations include Andrea Lopez-Luzuriaga at
> George Washington University's Center for Economic Research.

Drops "founded by researchers and policy experts" plural fiction. Names
GWU affiliation explicitly. 10K-hours-science-comm is distinctive and
supports the translation positioning. Verify the museum name — session
mentioned science museum, "explosions for the general public," Bill
Nye/Beakman style, but the museum's actual name wasn't captured.

## What to push back on

If Dan asks you to:

- **Soften the "What We Believe" block further.** Don't. Overly-polite
  analytical centrism loses what makes Canary distinct. Catastrophic
  risk is a real position the org should hold visibly.
- **Keep the CAIS extinction-statement pull-quote as the lede.** Push
  back firmly. This is the specific thing costing reach. It can appear
  in the body of the "What We Believe" block, not as the org's opening
  salvo.
- **Add additional signatory names beyond Hinton/Bengio/Altman/Amodei/
  Hassabis.** The 12-name list on the current site creates a chorus
  effect that reads as advocacy rhetoric rather than analytical claim.
  Five is enough.
- **Claim co-authors / collaborators more broadly than the actual
  relationships.** GWU via Lopez-Luzuriaga is real. Corda is a
  fellowship, not institutional backing. Don't inflate.
