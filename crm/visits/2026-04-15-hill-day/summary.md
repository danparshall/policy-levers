## Lessons from today (for next visit prep)

1. **I underweighted Rounds** — he's an active AI legislator (AI Caucus co-chair, just intro'd Workforce Commission Act with Warner). Walked in cold; recovered via post-visit email plan. Pre-visit prep should always cross-reference the senator's recent bill activity, not just committee membership.

2. **Original room numbers had errors** — Warner was at SH-703, not SR-475 as the original list said. Young was SD-185, not SH-B40. Lost ~15 min of walking. Verify against senate.gov authoritative source before walking.

3. **Front-desk warm contacts matter more than expected** — Daniela (Heinrich), Caleb (Hagerty UTK), Crystal (Hawley) all generated material follow-ups beyond the formal contact card. Treat reception as substantive, not just a checkpoint.

4. **MD constituent angle is undervalued in pre-planning** — Alsobrooks should have been Tier 1, not opportunistic. Constituent + freshman + AI-engaged staff = highest-leverage pattern.

5. **Workforce Commission Act + AIRE = legislative package** — discovered mid-day. This pairing is a coherent story to tell across multiple offices. Should be central to all Tier 1 D follow-ups (Warner, Blumenthal, Heinrich, Kaine).
