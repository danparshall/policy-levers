## What Happened After 5:01 PM

- **Trump** posted on Truth Social ~1 hour before the deadline, ordering all federal agencies to "immediately cease" using Anthropic's technology. Called Anthropic "Leftwing nut jobs."
- **Hegseth** formally designated Anthropic a **supply chain risk to national security** — first time this designation has ever been applied to an American company.
- **OpenAI** announced a classified network deal with the Pentagon **within hours** of the ban. CEO Altman claimed the deal includes the same safety red lines Anthropic had insisted on, while simultaneously agreeing to "all lawful purposes" language — the exact formulation Anthropic refused.
- **Anthropic** said the designation is "legally unsound" and announced it will **challenge in court**.
- **Senate Armed Services Committee** members sent a private letter urging resolution (Wicker's committee — leave-behind may have contributed).
- **Warner** condemned Trump's action, warning about "steering contracts to a preferred vendor."
- **Six-month phaseout** ordered for all government Anthropic usage.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Friday WFH is real** — thin staffing across the board. Tuesday-Thursday visits would yield more face time.
2. **Unplanned stops matter** — the Tillis thank-you visit cost five minutes and produced a military LA contact and a relationship with a staffer in the only Republican office publicly on record.
3. **Referrals are gold** — the Steube → Araboghli referral is the highest-value outcome of the day. A warm intro to a national security advisor on the Intelligence Committee from a Republican office is rare.
4. **Republican framing works** — contract sanctity, executive overreach, and defense industrial base arguments landed better than safety/civil liberties framing in R offices.
5. **The "too busy" brush-off from Raskin's office is worth pushing past** — he's the Ranking Member of Judiciary, Dan is a constituent who worked at NIST in his district, and this is squarely in his jurisdiction. Try again through a different channel.
6. **Same-day follow-up emails were sent** to every staffer who provided a card. This matters more than office #11.
